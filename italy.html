Italy

I had the privelage of visiting both Rome and Florence in Italy.


I must say one of my regrets was not properly researching Italy before visiting it. As a result, I felt I didn't get the most I could have gotten out of my trip.


The standout dish for me was a close tie. The first dish was a steak in Florence that was served with a balsamic vinegar reduction. A combination I would have never thought of. The tantilizing flavour of vinegar made the steak taste even better. I would describe it as a cross between eating steak and salt & vinegar chips.


That delicious steak was in a tie with a tripe dish also from Florence. I know what many of you are thinking: tripe? what is tripe?. And the rest of you are thinking: ugh no! Not tripe! Yes tripe comes from the stomach of either a pig or cow, but with most unconventional dishes: keep an open mind and it matters how its prepared. This tripe was deep fried, like calamari, then baked with a thick tomato sauce and topped with parmaesan cheese. Yum!
